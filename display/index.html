Serious Weather Events

Climate change increases the frequency and severity of storms and other major weather events. Arctic oscillation can cause XXXXcold snaps (?) while XXXXweather phenomena brings bigger nor'easters and hurricanes. Extreme weather impacts our infrastructure and transit isn't ready. Boston's MBTA is already underfunded, delayed maintenance puts stress on an old and heavily used system. Climate change will put increasing pressure on the MBTA, which over a million rely on daily for transportation every day. Delays and service interruptions will become more and more frequent, disrupting commuters' lives. Image Source: LolMBTA

Transit Delays on a (very) cold day

Storms are an obvious cause of transit delays and damage to key transit infrastructure, but other types of extreme weather can cause problems too. On December 28, 2017 the MBTA trains and buses were slowed by mechanical malfunctions on a bitterly cold day, temps were in the single digits and it caused major problems along multiple routes. Both weather and commuter volume impacts transit, so the worst delays cluster around peak commuting hours.

Transit Delays and Vulnerability

The relationship between the impact of climate change induced transit delays and a neighborhood's vulnerability isn't as clear cut as we had expected: the red line is an older system and it had pretty major delays on December 28th and it's a relatively wealthy corridor. However, transit delays still hurt low income commuters the most. People who are paid hourly may lose income or valuable time with their family when the MBTA gets delayed.
